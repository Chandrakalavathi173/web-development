ADVENTURE DESTINATIONS

Rishikesh

Rishikesh is located on the banks of the Ganga River in the northern state of Uttarakhand. The city is mostly a pilgrimage center and is also known as the yoga capital of India. There are, however, certain adventure activities conducted here for thrill-seeking tourists. You can engage in activities such as rafting in the Ganga River, rock and cliff climbing, take part in a survival camp at the nearby forest or go bungee jumping. The bungee jump is the highest in India at a staggering 83 meters high-leaving you in freefall for a whole 5 seconds.

Things to do: White water rafting, bungee jumping in Rishikesh Valley

Bir-Billing

Bir-Billing is a village settled in the Joginder Valley of Himachal Pradesh. The place portrays a typical and idyllic village setting. However, very close to the village you will find an entirely different picture. The entire sky is filled with paragliders enjoying this adventure sport. The hills and the climate make Bir-Billing the perfect spot to go paragliding. It is for this reason Bir-Billing is called the paragliding capital of the nation.

Things to do: Paragliding in the Bir Valley

Ladakh

Ladakh is known as the land of many monasteries. The town is located at the highest altitude in India. The topography of the surrounding land makes it the perfect place to go trekking and rafting. The famous Chadar Trek, which is a trek on a frozen river, takes place here. You can go rafting in the beautiful Zanskar Valley.

Things to do: Trek over the frozen river, rafting in Zanskar Valley

Andaman Islands

Andaman Islands are the union territory of India located to the south of the country. There are many water-based activities conducted here. You can engage in activities like snorkeling in the crystal clear waters or discover the various coral life forms and sea life when you go scuba diving. There are shipwrecks, too, to explore underwater.

Things to do: Diving and snorkelling in the crystal clear waters

Gulmarg

Gulmarg is located in the Baramulla district of Jammu and Kashmir. The setting and surroundings will remind you of a typical Switzerland village. The entire town is located at a high elevation and faces heavy snowfall that covers the entire village most of the time. Due to the snowfall received here, heli-skiing is a popular adventure sport. Heli-skiing involves jumping out of a helicopter on skis and face the harsh old winds.

Things to do: Heli-Skiing in the beautiful village

BEACH DESTINATIONS

Goa

Say beaches and the first place that comes to mind is Goa. Yes, Goa is one of the most popular beach destinations in the country, thanks to the many breath-taking beaches it has. Apart from the golden sands, palm and coconut trees and delectable seafood, the beaches here are famous for hosting thrilling water sports and amazing late-night parties. Goa is divided into two parts - North Goa and South Goa. While North Goa beaches are frequented by water sports and adventure enthusiasts, South Goa beaches are perfect for those looking to relax. So, plan your itinerary accordingly.

Things to Do: Swimming, parasailing, water scooter ride, kayaking, snorkelling, canoeing, scuba diving, meditation, yoga, shopping

Kochi

Popularly known as the 'Queen of the Arabian Sea', Kochi or Cochin is one of the best beach destinations in India. This small coastal city is peppered with picturesque beaches that serve as perfect retreat from urban din. The long seashore offers scenic views of the clear seawater and tranquil surroundings, bringing you closer to nature. From golden sands and awe-inspiring lagoons to old lighthouses and lush green coconut trees, the beaches here have everything you need. You can also enjoy a range of adventure activities on Kochi beaches. If you are fond of swimming, Cherai Beach is among the best beaches for swimming in India.

Things to Do: Boating, dolphin sighting, sea surfing, water skiing, parasailing

Lakshadweep Islands

Lakshadweep, the smallest union territory in India, has 36 islands and some of the most stunning beaches. Apart from unique natural beauty, the islands have many amazing spots that attract tourists from far and wide. Sandy beaches, beautiful lagoons, and plenty of flora and fauna is what makes Lakshadweep a must-visit. Surrounded by the Arabian Sea, the islands are devoid of any hustle-bustle. They are also known for some of the best beaches in India for honeymooners, thanks to the absence of loud crowds. You may have to take a helicopter or boat to travel from one island to another.

Things to Do: Swimming, scuba diving, sailing, angling and boating in glass-bottom boats near Kavaratti Beach, birdwatching

Chennai

Home to many famous beaches in India, the beautiful city of Chennai is a traveller’s treat. Sun-kissed golden sands, undulating ocean waves and soothing sea breeze add to the beauty of the coastlines. Marina Beach, which is the longest beach in India and the second-longest in the world, is in Chennai. The sight of the evening sun from the beach, slowly disappearing into the ocean, is an awesome experience. You can also explore the many tourist attractions and shopping malls in this metro city.

Things to Do: Swimming, relishing murukku, raw mango, sundal and other local delicacies

Vishakapatnam

Vizag or Visakhapatnam is a bustling coastal city that has plenty of things to offer the tourist for relaxation. However, only few people know that Vizag is also an ultimate beach holiday destination of India. It is an ideal place to enjoy relaxed evenings while strolling down the beaches. There are plenty of resorts also available near the beach to have a beautiful stay. For enjoying the panoramic view of the sea, you can visit the place.

Things to Do: Swimming, windsurfing, jet skiing, speed boating, snorkelling, scuba diving, visiting Kursura Submarine Museum near Rama Krishna Beach, trying seafood and street food

ECO DESTINATIONS

Honey hills,Kerala

Ecotourisms are the fast developing tourism segments of Kerala. Thenmala or Honey Hills is the unique destination in Kerala. The destination is known for its ecotourism and beautiful landscapes which are not only comforting but also stimulating. Located about 72kms from Thiruvananthapuram, the Thenmala Eco-Tourism stocks its resources with the well-known Shenduruney Wildlife Sanctuary at the slopes of the Western Ghats.

Chilika,Orissa

This is a biodiversity advert and the major brackish water lake in Asia. Furthermore, the largest lagoon along India’s east coast spreads over 1100 sqkm. A boat drive in this lagoon will get you amid the squeaks of oodles of migratory birds from Baikal, Siberia, South-east Asia, and Central Asia. Lakhs of migratory birds flock here during winter every year. Along with the migratory birds, more than a few species of local birds are also farsighted at Mangalajodi all through the year and a number of of them nest in the abode.

Khangchendzonga,sikkim

Known as Kanchenjunga biosphere reserve. It is a national park and biosphere located in the Sikkim state of India. It is also included in UNESCO under the “Man and Biosphere programme”. The park acquired its name from the Kanchenjunga mountain, the third highest peak in the world. It has several flora and fauna. Flora includes plenty of oak trees, fir, maple, birch, willow. Shrubs and other medicinal herbs. Fauna includes Snow Leopard, Himalayan Black bear, red panda, musk deer, sloth bear, rat snake, Russell’s viper, Himalayan blue sheep one etc. Things can do in the park include several activities like trekking, the department, and travel agents organising trekking.

Chail,Himachal Pradesh

Chail is a hill station located in Himachal Pradesh. Chail is also frequently visited by hikers as this area provides a beautiful vision of the lower Himalayas. Many eco camps are located here. Tourist attractions here are Chail Sanctuary, Chail Gurudwara, Lover’s point, Stone Kumbd, and Sadupul, a small village with a river restaurant. There are water park and restaurant too.

Kodaikanal,Tamilnadu

Located in Dindigul district, Tamil Nadu. Known for it are mind-blowing beauty and lakes. Ecotourism in Kodaikanal includes visiting Guna caves, Kodai Lakes, Berijam lake, there are few waterfalls. Tourist activities are boating, hiking, cycling, horse riding, fishing.

CULTURAL DESTINATIONS

Amristar

Amritsar is undoubtedly the heart of the Sikh culture in the country which has been recognised as a holy town too by Guru Ram Das, the 4th Sikh guru. In fact, locations such as Jallianwala Bagh, Wagah Border, Akal Takht and the very famous Sri Harmandir Sahib (Golden Temple) best express the rich and flourishing Sikh culture. Amritsar has many gurudwaras, museums and cultural places that make it one of the best places to experience the Sikh culture. Not to mention how exquisite and delicious the cuisine is with Chhole Bhatures and Amritsari Kulchas!

Tamilnadu

From Madurai's Meenakshi Amman Temple to the monolithic structures and blissful rock carvings at Mahabalipuram, Tamil Nadu is a land of exquisite cultural heritage. The beautiful South Indian state is known for its wondrous temples, Carnatic music celebrations, gorgeous architecture and much more. The illustrious folklore and tales that you will witness wherever you go in Tamil Nadu are going to be just as fascinating as the structures itself. Besides, teeming the cultural tourism of the state with the mind-blowing cuisine and you've got yourself quite the cultural trip.

Hampi

While Mysore does have some cultural reference of the Vijaynagar empire but to get the most of its origin, you should visit Hampi. The gorgeous city is a world heritage site for housing the ruins of the ancient Vijaynagar kingdom. The Dravidian-style architecture and art collections will enthral you. Right from the Krishna temple complex to the Pattabhirama temple complex, there are many such structures with cultural relevance that would amaze you. Hampi is most certainly one of the most beautiful places in India. Plus, the cuisine of the place further makes it a place you should visit at least once in your lifetime.

Mysore

Mysore has been considered to be the cultural capital of Karnataka and for all the right reasons. With palaces galore and myriad of museums and galleries, you would be amazed by the rich culture of the city. In fact, Mysore lights up during various festivals and celebrates with utmost gusto. In the past, the city witnessed rulers from the Vijayanagar and Mughal Empire so much of the cultural heritage that we speak of are inspired by these times. When you visit the city, do check out the Amba Vilas Palace, better known as Mysore Palace, Chamundi Hilltop Temple, Jayalakshmi Vilas Mansion and more to relish the fabulous cultures of Mysore.

Lucknow

With an architecture that is heavily influenced by the Mughals, the Delhi Sultanate, the Nawabs of Awadh and the British, you can imagine how magnificent the heritage and culture of Lucknow is. It's exquisite artwork and takes on craftsmanship further add its the city's charm and its cultural heritage. From Begum Hazrat Mahal Park's beauty to Rumi Darwaza's dramatic setting to the British Residency Complex, these places in Lucknow cannot be missed. Perhaps that is why it is also called the land of Nawabs. You would surely be amazed by Lucknow's tourist attractions as one of the cultural places in India.

WILDLIFE DESTINATIONS

Jim Corbett National Park-Uttarkhand

Jim Corbett National Park wildlife safari in India is also one of the most exciting safaris. Situated in the mountainous district of Nainital, Jim Corbett National Park which is a part of the larger Corbett Tiger reserve is home to a large number of tigers. If you are lucky, you may spot a white tiger as well. This is one of the most popular wildlife destinations in India.

Things to do: Drive around the park to catch a glimpse of the wildlife, try angling at Kosi River, visit the Corbett falls

Ranthambore National Park-Rajasthan

Ranthambore National Park wildlife safari in India is one of the most adventurous safaris. This National Park is located in Rajasthan and was previously a famous hunting ground for the Maharajas. Now a wildlife conservation park, this park is most famous for its Tiger trails and Bird watching tours.

Things to do: Bakula has a lot of waterholes and hence is crowded with wildlife. The Raj Bagh ruins are ancient ruined structures, domes and arches. The Padam Talao is another lake worth visiting.

Kaziranga National Park-Assam

This park has the largest population of one horned Rhino in the World. This national park has achieved great success with its conservation projects.

Things to do: Check out the tea plantations around the park Go on a safari to spot rhinos and tigers, Take in a view of the entire park from the watchtowers

Bandhavgarh National Park-Madhya Pradesh

This Park has a large population of tigers and langurs. The park is at the center of India and is home to the famous white tiger.

Things to do: Go for a birding tour or a jeep safari to catch a glimpse of the best wildlife. Also once there, do not miss out on seeing the Khajuraho temples. Also, have a look at the Bandhavgarh fort.

Bhitarkanika National Park - Odisha

Located in the Kendarapa district of Odisha, Bhitarkanika National Park is the core area of the Bhitarkanika Wildlife Sanctuary. Covering an area of 145 km sq., this place is dense mangrove forest with salt-tolerant trees. The marshy land and various big and small water streams provide the best habitat to crocodiles, lizards, and snakes.

Things to do: Go boat cruising, angling or beaching at the Pentha Sea Beach
